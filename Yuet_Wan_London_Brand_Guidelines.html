The Idea

Brand Essence

品牌核心 · 硯 · 臺 · 城

Yuet Wan London exists to keep a centuries-old voice singing in a modern city. We carry Cantonese opera — a UNESCO-recognised art of brush, breath and movement — from the inkstones of Lingnan to the stages of London, so the diaspora feels at home and the city hears something it has never heard before.

Our philosophy

傳情以藝，化人以文
Move hearts through art; cultivate people through culture.

Everything we make should do one of these two things — stir a feeling, or pass something on. If a poster, a programme or a post does neither, it isn't ours yet.

Logo System

標誌系統

The brush characters 粵韻 are the heart of the brand. Three lockups extend them for different jobs. Never redraw the calligraphy — it is the one fixed asset everything else is built around.

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space of at least the height of the 韻 character on every side. Never place the mark below 22 mm wide in print, or 90 px on screen — below this the brushwork closes up.

韻韻

The icon · 粵韻

When space is tight — avatars, app icons, favicons, stamps — use the brush mark alone. It is the most recognisable, most ownable asset we have. Pair it with a single colour ground (Ink, Paper or Vermilion).

Misuse — never do this

Don't stretch or squash
Don't rotate or tilt
Don't place on clashing colour

Working ratio

White / Paper — 60% Ink — 24% Vermilion 9% Brass 7%

Vermilion is a spice, not a sauce. Use it for one decisive moment per layout — a seal, a title, a rule. Jade and Rouge appear only in small supporting roles. Brass is for lines and ornament, rarely as a fill. White is the primary light ground; Rice Paper adds warmth in small doses. Ink is the primary dark ground; Night Indigo is an optional cooler tone when a layout needs variety.

The Toolkit

Graphic Language

輔助圖形 · 取於臺

One device anchors the system — a window-lattice pattern lifted straight from the hexagon badge. Used as a quiet texture behind text, on end-papers and inner covers, it gives every layout a family resemblance even when the logo isn’t present.

Brass on Rice Paper
Paper on Ink
Window lattice窗花

The six-sided window from the hexagon badge, repeated into a quiet texture. Use it on end-papers, programme inner covers, badge linings, and as a faint watermark behind text.

Keep it a whisper

Always low-contrast and large in scale — brass on paper, or pale on ink. The lattice supports the page; it is never the loudest thing on it.

聲06

How We Speak

Voice & Tone

語氣風格

We write like a proud, generous host — someone who loves this art form and wants you to love it too. Warm, never academic. Clear in two languages. We translate the feeling, not just the words.

Welcome first

Assume no prior knowledge. Invite, then explain.

Feeling over facts

Lead with the story and the emotion; let detail follow.

Equal in 中文

English and Chinese carry the same warmth — neither is an afterthought.

Proud, not precious

Honour the tradition without gatekeeping it.

We say

“Never seen Cantonese opera? This is the perfect place to start.”

“A thousand-year-old story, sung live, with surtitles in English and 中文.”

“Seniors 60+ — half price, always. This is your music too.”

We don't say

“An exhibition of subaltern intangible heritage praxis.” — cold and academic.

“For connoisseurs of authentic Yueju only.” — gatekeeping.

“Cheap tickets!!!” — loud, off-brand, no dignity.

則07

One Page To Remember

Quick Rules

速覽守則

Always

  • Lead with the brush mark 粵韻 — it's the most ownable asset.
  • Give English and 中文 equal weight and warmth.
  • Keep one decisive vermilion moment per layout.
  • Use brass for fine lines and ornament, not big fills.
  • Close important pieces with the seal / chop.
  • Honour the clear space and minimum sizes.

Never

  • Redraw, recolour or re-space the calligraphy.
  • Stretch, rotate or add effects to the logo.
  • Flood a layout with vermilion or gradients.
  • Add shadows, glows or 3-D effects to any mark.
  • Write in a cold, academic or gatekeeping tone.
  • Mix in unrelated typefaces or clip-art.
